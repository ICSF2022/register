Our Introduction

About Our Organization

Akhil Bhatkya Vimukta Jati Jamati Mahasangh is a pioneering organization dedicated to the welfare and empowerment of nomadic tribes (Vimukta Jati) across Maharashtra. Our movement represents the collective voice of communities that have historically faced marginalization and social exclusion.

We strive to create awareness about our cultural heritage while addressing contemporary challenges through political engagement, educational initiatives, and economic upliftment programs.

Community Support

Providing legal aid, counseling, and social support services to community members

Education

Scholarship programs and educational initiatives for youth empowerment

Advocacy

Fighting for political representation and policy changes at state and national levels

Our President - Bhibishant Jadhav

Leadership That Inspires

Shri Bhibishant Jadhav, our esteemed President, has been at the forefront of the movement for nomadic tribe rights for over three decades. Coming from a humble background himself, he has dedicated his life to uplifting marginalized communities.

Under his leadership, the Mahasangh has grown from a small collective to a powerful organization with representation across Maharashtra. His vision of social equality through education and economic empowerment has transformed countless lives.

"Our strength lies in our unity. When we stand together as one community, no challenge is too great to overcome. The saffron of our courage and the green of our growth will light the way forward."

- Bhibishant Jadhav

Our Journey

A timeline of our organization's milestones and achievements

1995 - Foundation

The Mahasangh was founded by Bhibishant Jadhav and a group of community elders to address the challenges faced by nomadic tribes in Maharashtra.

2002 - First Major Rally

Organized a historic rally in Mumbai with over 10,000 participants, bringing national attention to nomadic tribe issues.

2008 - Educational Initiatives

Launched scholarship programs for nomadic tribe students, enabling hundreds to pursue higher education.

2014 - Political Representation

Successfully advocated for reserved seats for nomadic tribes in local governance bodies.

2019 - Economic Programs

Initiated skill development and microfinance programs benefiting over 5,000 families.

2023 - Current Reach

Now active in all districts of Maharashtra with over 50,000 registered members and 200 local chapters.

Our Inspiration

Roots of Our Movement

Our organization draws inspiration from centuries of nomadic tribe resilience and the visionary leaders who fought for our community's dignity. The traditions of mutual aid and collective decision-making in our culture form the foundation of our organizational ethos.

We honor the legacy of freedom fighters from nomadic communities who contributed to India's independence struggle, often without recognition.

Core Principles

Community First

Every decision is made with the welfare of our community members as the top priority.

Social Justice

We fight for equal rights and opportunities for all nomadic communities.

Cultural Preservation

We document and promote our rich cultural heritage and traditions.

Collective Growth

We believe in the principle of 'progress together' - no one gets left behind.
